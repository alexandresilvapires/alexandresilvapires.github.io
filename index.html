About Me

Hey! I'm Alexandre Pires, a Ph.D. student at the Prosocial Dynamics Lab, part of the Socially Intelligent Artificial Systems (SIAS) Group, University of Amsterdam. My work focuses on understanding indirect reciprocity as a mechanism for cooperation under Human-AI hybrid populations. I'm supervised by Fernando P. Santos (SIAS) and Ana Paiva (GAIPS). The focus of my formal education is on artificial intelligence and complex systems. However, I always strive to bring perspectives from various fields, such as music, art, history, economics, ecology and more.

Before this, I did my Computer Science and Engineering master's degree at Instituto Superior Técnico, University of Lisbon, Portugal, supervised by professors Francisco C. Santos and Jorge M. Pacheco.

In addition to my work in computer science, I study jazz piano, video-game development, languages, and more. I'm also part of the jazz-fusion band Frog Jumps Out.

Here you can find all my projects!

The Prosocial Dynamics Lab

What I'm doing

  • Research

    My scientific focus is studying cooperation in human-AI hybrid populations, merging game theoretical models and experimental research. Other topics include complex systems, multi-agent systems, and pro-social AI.

  • Jazz and Composition

    I study jazz performance, composition and arrangement. My main instruments are piano and saxophone. I'm the pianist, keyboardist and composer of the jazz-fusion band Frog Jumps Out.

  • Video-games

    I love all facets of video-game development, from programming to design, music, and art! My first large project, LIMITATIONS, is currently in development.

  • Other activities

    I do all sorts of other things, from language learning (currently, JP, IT and NL), poetry, digital art, photography and more!
